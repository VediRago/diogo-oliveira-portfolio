# Emotional Architecture

Noeme is designed around emotional pacing and breathing room.

Structure matters, but the audience needs time to care before the story makes them hurt.

## Emotional Rhythm

**Breathing Room:** Space to settle into characters and relationships before pressure rises.

**Pressure:** Tension narrowing toward danger, loss, or change.

**Rupture:** The point where rhythm breaks and emotional safety is disrupted.

**Overload:** Tension beyond normal scene rhythm. Perception becomes unstable.

**Stillness:** Aftermath space where impact can be felt.

## Pacing Pattern

Early acts build breathing room.

Pressure rises gradually, then ruptures suddenly.

Mid-acts shift between stillness and escalating urgency.

Late acts push toward overload, where multiple storylines and perspectives collapse into each other, then end in stillness.

## Why It Matters

The audience should not feel constantly pressured.

They need attachment before loss. They need stillness after impact.

This timing also helps manage [[Mystery Debt]]. Rewards and revelations need to arrive when the audience needs them, not just when the plot is ready to reveal them.
