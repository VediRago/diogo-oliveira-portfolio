# Reader Reward

← Back to [Treasure Key Treasure](Treasure_Key_Treasure.md)

The reader is not owed every answer immediately.

The reader is owed value for their attention.

That value can come through emotional attachment, a meaningful image, a character moment, a working explanation, a question that leads somewhere, recognition later, or a new layer of understanding.

## Core Rule

The story should not ask readers to wait empty-handed.

Every act should give them something real to hold before the next stretch begins.

## Why It Matters

Mystery creates pressure.

If a story only adds questions, the audience eventually becomes frustrated. Reader reward keeps the experience alive by giving partial satisfaction before expanding the frame again.

The audience continues because the story keeps giving value before asking for more patience.
