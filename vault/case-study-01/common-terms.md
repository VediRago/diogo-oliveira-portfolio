# Common Terms

## Core Historical Terms

### Viriatus

The official mythic name of the displaced founder.

Viriatus is the child from another human civilization who arrives in Roman history and becomes the historical hinge of Erit's world.

House Ventari hides his true arrival, trains him, teaches him, shapes him, and uses him as an asset for control.

After Ecalus dies, Viriatus's living public meaning threatens the siblings' control of what comes next because people could gather around him as the figure for the reshaped Rome.

Ilunis kills Viriatus privately after Ermerus turns jealousy into duty.

Ermerus then turns Viriatus's death into public grief, ceremony, and the foundation of Regulatus.

Earlier historical layers may remember Viriatus only as the unnamed founder, the sacred death, or the statue figure.

The name Viriatus becomes attached later as the official mythic name.

### Celcinus

An older civic founder figure connected to the pre-Regulatus calendar.

Celcinus does not need to be a major character yet.

His purpose is to show that Erit's Roman-derived world already had an older order and calendar before House Ventari rewrote time around Viriatus's death.

### ACD

The older calendar era used before Regulatus and before the AVD reset.

ACD is tied to the Celcinus Calendar and the pre-Regulatus order.

Exact meaning is still provisional, but functionally it marks time before House Ventari rewrites history around Viriatus's death.

### AVD — After Viriatus's Death

The new calendar era declared when Ermerus turns Viriatus's death into the public foundation of Regulatus.

AVD begins when Regulatus comes into play as the new era, not merely at the private moment Viriatus dies.

Public meaning:

after Viriatus's noble death gave civilization order.

Forbidden meaning:

after House Ventari used Viriatus's murder to replace the old order with Regulatus.

## Houses and Lines

### House Ventari

The original ruling house that concealed Viriatus's arrival, trained him, taught him, shaped him, and used him as an asset for control.

House Ventari uses Viriatus's victories, public grief after his death, and public displeasure with the old order to replace that old order with Regulatus.

House Ventari knows the public meaning of Regulatus is constructed.

They perform belief while understanding its function as control.

They are the origin of the four founding lines: Ermerus Ventari, Rutare Ventari, Ilunis Ventari, and Teresta Ventari.

### Ecalus Ventari

The Ventari father.

Ecalus begins by using Viriatus as a weapon, proof, and advantage.

Near death, he begins seeing Viriatus as the son he wished he had.

He dies before creating the next order.

Ermerus takes the reins after him.

### Ermerus Ventari

One of the four founding siblings.

Associated with meaning, charm, narrative control, legitimacy, reputation, public memory, diplomacy, and the official interpretation of history.

Ermerus manipulates Ilunis into killing Viriatus, then turns public grief into the foundation of Regulatus.

### Rutare Ventari

One of the four founding siblings.

Associated with order, administration, civic records, procedure, permissions, identity status, and systems that decide who belongs.

### Ilunis Ventari

One of the four founding siblings.

Associated with force, law, judgment, military authority, punishment, security, and violence framed as duty.

### Teresta Ventari

One of the four founding siblings.

Associated with continuity, study, preservation, medicine, bodily control, augmentation, chips, and the long path toward House Devaar.

### House Devaar

The dominant house in Erit's era.

House Devaar descends from the Teresta line and becomes powerful through preservation medicine, augmentation, chip systems, bodily identification, and control over treatment access.

Where House Ventari founded the ideological order, House Devaar becomes powerful by controlling the systems people physically depend on.

House Devaar should not feel like a random powerful family.

It is the later result of Teresta's rejected line returning to power through medicine, dependency, body control, and preservation.

## Civilizational Terms

### Empiran

The Roman-derived political civilization of Erit's era.

The word comes from language drift from Imperium, but should feel like its own term rather than a direct copy of Rome.

Empiran refers to the political civilization created after House Ventari uses Viriatus's public legacy, Regulatus, the founding lines, and later dynastic systems to replace the old Roman-derived order.

Viriatus does not knowingly create Empiran as a political system.

His victories, image, and stolen death become the material House Ventari uses to build it.

Empiran should not necessarily name the entire shared world-line of Lucreta, Sera, Erit, and the hospital girl.

### Regulatus

The inherited doctrine of ordered legacy.

On the surface, Regulatus teaches duty, pride, gratitude, maturity, family honor, hierarchy, and belonging.

Underneath, it converts pride into obedience and keeps power flowing toward the ruling structures.

Most citizens do not experience Regulatus as oppression.

They experience it as civilization.

### Celcinus Calendar

The older Roman-derived civic calendar connected to Celcinus and the pre-Regulatus order.

It exists to show that time was already being counted before House Ventari reset or absorbed history through the AVD system.

## Social Terms

### Civel

Language drift from Cives.

A civic person, citizen, or recognized member of the ordered society.

Exact legal meaning still needs to be developed.

### Servan

Language drift from Servus / Servi.

A servile, worker, or owned/service-bound class.

Servan are important because Viriatus is first found by people beneath the ruling class, and their testimony becomes dangerous.

Exact status levels still need development.

### Patren

Language drift from Patricii.

Likely connected to old noble or elite civic families.

Needs development once class structure becomes clearer.

## Place / Institution Terms

### Ludanis

Language drift from Ludus.

Potentially connected to training schools, arenas, gladiator systems, or formal discipline spaces.

Needs development.

### Doma

Language drift from Domus.

Likely connected to house, household, family estate, or formal domestic structure.

Needs development.

### Colossia

Language drift from Colosseum.

Likely connected to arena spectacle, gladiators, public punishment, entertainment, or civic ritual.

Needs development.

### Legian

Language drift from Legio.

Likely connected to military forces, especially structures tied to Ilunis authority.

Needs development.

### Senat

Language drift from Senatus.

Likely connected to council, government, old civic authority, or formal political body.

Needs development.

## Design Rule

A term should not exist only because it sounds good.

A term should eventually connect to history, social pressure, behavior, atmosphere, quest logic, perception change, or hidden treasure.

If a term cannot connect to pressure or meaning, it should stay provisional.
