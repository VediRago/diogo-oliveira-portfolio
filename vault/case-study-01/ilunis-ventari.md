# Ilunis Ventari

Ilunis Ventari is the second son of [House Ventari](house-ventari.md).

Where [Rutare Ventari](rutare-ventari.md) is protected because he is heir, Ilunis is overused because he is useful. He is strong, sent into danger, and treated as someone who can survive what others cannot.

At the Battle of Augunar, Ilunis is maimed while defending Ventari lands. His lower body is permanently damaged, leaving him barely able to walk. The injury does not only take his mobility; it takes the proof of worth House Ventari taught him to rely on.

Ilunis believes he gave his body to the family and still lost his place beside [Ecalus Ventari](ecalus-ventari.md). [Viriatus](viriatus.md) becomes the living proof that sacrifice did not earn Ilunis what he wanted.

To Ilunis, the wound is simple:

**I gave my body to this family, and Father gave his pride to someone else.**

Rather than blame House Ventari for using him, Ilunis turns his pain outward. Mercy, softness, and hesitation become enemy ideas. If pain was necessary, then his suffering was not meaningless. If force is order, then his damaged body can still belong to strength.

After Ecalus dies, [Ermerus Ventari](ermerus-ventari.md) gives Ilunis's jealousy the shape of duty. The idea is simple and dangerous:

**Viriatus is not their blood.**

That thought lets Ilunis see murder not as resentment, but as protection of House Ventari.

During family mourning, while Viriatus kneels over Ecalus's casket as a grieving son, Ilunis stabs him in the back. The murder is not battlefield glory. It is a private betrayal committed against someone grieving the same father.

Ilunis becomes the hand that commits the crime Ermerus turns into history.

His descendants later become tied to law, punishment, judgment, military authority, and sanctioned violence. Their contradiction is that they punish betrayal while descending from betrayal.
