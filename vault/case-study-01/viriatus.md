# Viriatus

Viriatus is one of the most consequential figures in Erit's documented history, though the public account of his life obscures its foundational truths.

## Origin and Arrival

Appearing around eleven or in his early teens, Viriatus arrives from another human civilization and is discovered by [Servan](common-terms.md) before being taken to [House Ventari](house-ventari.md). His true age cannot be verified. His origin world has lost natural family and biological inheritance entirely—a wound he carries into his new life without language, status, or protection.

The discovery is publicly reframed. Where [Servan](common-terms.md) says he fell from the heavens, House Ventari claims it shaped him. This distinction matters because it allows House Ventari to control the narrative of his meaning from the beginning.

## Function and Achievement

Raised by House Ventari, Viriatus is trained into a military force of uncommon capability. His victories help overthrow the old Roman-derived order and expand Ventari conquest beyond Rome itself. His public meaning becomes inseparable from proof that Ventari discipline works—that their method can create greatness.

His strength is not constant power. His body carries stress responses developed in harsher survival conditions, capable of extreme adrenaline bursts under threat or protective pressure. This makes him potent but vulnerable: he can be trained, shaped, and used precisely because he is not invulnerable.

## Why He Fights

Viriatus fights because several forces converge. He experiences genuine belonging from House Ventari—shelter, language, purpose, a father figure in [Ecalus Ventari](ecalus-ventari.md). Old Rome is framed as weak and unable to protect families. His displacement wound makes continuity and legacy feel sacred. Ecalus's affection becomes proof that his strength serves something meaningful.

The belief that becomes dangerous is this: order can protect what chaos destroys. From displacement, this belief makes sense. From conquest, it becomes justification.

## Public vs. Private Meaning

As his victories accumulate, people begin to see House Ventari as important because of Viriatus, rather than the reverse. After [Ecalus Ventari](ecalus-ventari.md)'s death, this reversal becomes threat. A living Viriatus could become the emotional center around which others gather—more powerful in public meaning than the siblings who inherited the house.

Publicly, Viriatus is beloved by soldiers, feared by enemies, and mythologized by common people. Privately, he does not know the Servans who brought him to safety were removed from record. He does not fully understand how his victories are being claimed as proof of Ventari authorship. He does not know his inability to create a bloodline makes him easier for House Ventari to use—and later, to eliminate.

## Death and Legacy

Viriatus is killed privately after [Ecalus Ventari](ecalus-ventari.md)'s death, murdered while grieving the father figure he loved. The killing is portrayed as noble death in war. [Ermerus Ventari](ermerus-ventari.md) converts the murder into public ceremony and turns the stolen meaning into the emotional foundation of [Regulatus](regulatus.md). His death marks the beginning of the [AVD calendar](avd-calendar.md).

Most citizens remember Viriatus as a founder and hero. Very few know the truth: that he was taken in, shaped, used, loved through usefulness, and finally eliminated when his living presence threatened inheritance.

## Tragic Structure

Viriatus wants family, belonging, and legacy—things his origin world already lost. House Ventari gives him those things in a form that can be controlled and eventually weaponized against him.

He cannot create his own bloodline. Another family turns his longing for continuity into their own inherited order. That is the wound beneath the conquest.
