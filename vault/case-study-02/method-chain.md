# Method Chain

The modular method begins with the smallest useful chain:

**Experience → Emotion → Reason → Behavior**

This explains how a person, group, or background element reacts to what has happened to them. It is enough for many characters, wounds, founders, siblings, and NPCs.

When repeated behavior begins shaping the feeling of a place, group, house, city, or institution, the chain expands:

**Experience → Emotion → Reason → Behavior → Atmosphere**

Atmosphere is earned when behavior becomes visible beyond one person. It is the emotional weather created by repeated behavior.

When atmosphere survives through time and becomes myth, law, record, ritual, institution, tradition, propaganda, or inherited structure, the chain expands again:

**Experience → Emotion → Reason → Behavior → Atmosphere → History**

History is old events turned into durable atmosphere.

When that history, atmosphere, or world pressure creates a playable problem, the chain becomes useful for quest design:

**Experience → Emotion → Reason → Behavior → Atmosphere → Quest Logic**

Quest logic is earned when the existing chain naturally creates needs, obstacles, choices, and consequences.

The chain should only expand when the next layer is earned. A character may only need the first four steps. A place may need atmosphere. An institution may need history. A quest may need the chain to reach playable pressure.

The method stays the same. The depth changes.
