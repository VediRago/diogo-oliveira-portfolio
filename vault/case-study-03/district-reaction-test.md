# District Reaction Test

This test checks whether a district can react to player action without making major factions lose their identity.

A district in Vys begins with a strong [Regulatus](../wiki/regulatus.md) layer. It has ration offices, records, permits, Scutis authority, and fear of disorder. It also carries a weaker Gang / Block layer because daily survival still depends on local people, local favors, and local protection.

Lux is present through an aid hall. Novacula exists only as a weak idea spreading through rumor. Virtus and Scutis remain as authority pressure.

The player exposes ration corruption inside the local administration. Citizens learn that the ration system was not simply failing; it was being abused by people trusted to protect it.

The district's Regulatus layer weakens. Gang / Block influence grows stronger. Novacula becomes easier to hear because the corruption gives its claims more weight. Lux receives more desperate visitors, but also becomes more suspicious because people begin asking who else uses help as control.

The locked factions do not change identity. Lux does not stop being Lux. Virtus does not stop being Virtus. Scutis command does not become Novacula.

Instead, they push back inside the changed district. Lux tries to calm people while keeping them dependent on aid. Scutis increases patrols. Virtus reads the district as another example of weakness spreading through the city.

The result is reactivity without faction collapse. The player changes local mood, trust, behavior, and atmosphere, but the major factions remain themselves.
