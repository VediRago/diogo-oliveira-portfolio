# Fluid and Locked Layers

Layered reactivity needs limits.

If every layer can change easily, the world stops feeling believable. If nothing can change, the world feels static.

Fluid layers are used for people and places that can shift through strong experience: citizens, districts, gangs, merchants, workers, local mood, and undecided NPCs. A district can lose trust in official records. A worker can become more open to Novacula. A crowd can become more willing to hide food, lie to Scutis, or rely on the block instead of the office.

Locked layers belong to institutions with strong identity, ritual, training, hierarchy, or doctrine. Lux does not stop being Lux because a district becomes angry. Virtus does not stop being Virtus because citizens lose trust. Scutis command does not become Novacula because the player exposes corruption.

Instead, locked layers become pressure points inside changed conditions.

The player may change how a city sees Lux, Virtus, Scutis, gangs, Novacula, or Regulatus. The player may change how citizens respond to them. But major factions keep their deeper identity.

This creates tension without breaking immersion.
