# NPC Archetypes

This file stores reusable NPC archetypes for the Dark Time game world around 2600 AVD.

These are not named characters. They are templates for building quest givers, witnesses, merchants, allies, enemies, and background citizens.

NPCs should be built using the project method:

**world pressure → character need → obstacle → possible approaches → consequence**

## Block Worker

Industrial laborer, maintenance worker, transport worker, refinery worker, or factory hand.

The Block Worker exists under overcrowding, wage stagnation, ration shortages, dangerous labor, and no realistic upward mobility. Their need is simple: keep themselves or their family alive. Their obstacle is that legal work barely provides enough food, water, or rent to survive.

They commonly appear with worn clothes, tired eyes, grease stains, work tools, a lunch tin, and old injuries. They are exhausted, practical, and resigned.

Common lines:

> Another day.
>
> Maybe next year.
>
> Work starts in twenty.
>
> Could be worse.

## Gang Runner

Messenger, lookout, smuggler, courier, or errand runner for a district gang.

The Gang Runner exists under poverty, gang territory, blocked legal paths, and the need for belonging. They need money, identity, protection, and purpose. Their obstacle is that leaving the gang may mean losing protection, income, and community.

They commonly appear with fast movements, nervous eyes, a cheap weapon, a hidden package, and a patched jacket. They are quick, ambitious, frightened, and loyal to the block.

Common lines:

> Need something moved?
>
> Didn't see anything.
>
> Wrong street, friend.
>
> Everybody works for somebody.

## Lux Volunteer

Aid worker, soup hall helper, shelter assistant, clinic aide, or burial helper.

The Lux Volunteer exists under endless hunger, sickness, grief, and poverty after the flare. They need to help people without losing faith in the work. Their obstacle is that there are always more people in need than Lux can feed, shelter, or heal.

They commonly appear in simple clothing, with a Lux mark, an exhausted smile, and clean hands made rough by work. They are kind, patient, and tired.

Common lines:

> Sit.
>
> Eat first.
>
> You look tired.
>
> The Light finds everyone eventually.

## Scutis Patrolman

Urban order enforcer and street-level arm of state authority.

The Scutis Patrolman exists under crime, riots, gang violence, black markets, and cities too large for ordinary governance. They need to maintain order before disorder spreads. Their obstacle is that citizens fear them, gangs test them, and every district hides more problems than procedure can solve.

They commonly appear in armored exosuits, riot plating, integrated communications, and heavy urban gear, never fully relaxed. They are suspicious, controlled, and certain.

Common lines:

> Identify yourself.
>
> Move along.
>
> Discipline or Grave.
>
> Compliance prevents casualties.

## Corner Merchant

Small trader, food seller, cigarette seller, junk dealer, rumor broker, or block shopkeeper.

The Corner Merchant exists under scarcity, gang taxes, ration instability, theft, and constant price changes. They need to stay open and stay alive. Their obstacle is that everything costs more, everyone wants credit, and every faction wants information.

They commonly appear behind a small barred shop, too many locks, an old counter, a hidden weapon, and half-empty shelves. They are watchful, cynical, and socially sharp.

Common lines:

> Cash first.
>
> Didn't hear that from me.
>
> Prices went up.
>
> Everything's expensive except bad news.

## Old Veteran

Former soldier, retired worker, old gladiator, former Virtus auxiliary, or survivor of earlier conflicts.

The Old Veteran exists under the memory of a better, or at least different, time; physical decline; and the pain of watching the city decay. They need to leave something behind before being forgotten. Their obstacle is that younger people are too tired to care, and institutions prefer clean official memory.

They commonly appear with old medals, missing teeth, a prosthetic limb, a worn coat, slow movements, and sharp eyes. They are bitter, observant, and unexpectedly protective.

Common lines:

> City used to breathe.
>
> Nobody remembers the sky.
>
> Everybody says they're saving the world.
>
> World doesn't want saving.
