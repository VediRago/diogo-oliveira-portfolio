# 2026-06-04 — Erit World Development Diary

## Main Purpose

Today began the Erit worldbuilding branch as a support structure for Noeme.

The goal was to see whether details already present in the story could grow into a deeper world without damaging the main narrative.

The working rule became:

> Story first. Systems after.

## Main Focus Areas

### Fit Map

The first question was how to make the existing details connect.

The early chain linked Sera's statue, the displaced founder, Regulatus, dynastic lines, House Devaar, Erit, and the later conflict around identity, medicine, power, and trust.

This became the first support chain for Erit's world.

### Displaced Founder

The world needed a person before it needed a map.

The displaced child became the foundation node: someone from a more advanced civilization entering Roman history and being interpreted through myth, prophecy, politics, and divine legitimacy.

This made the world's divergence personal rather than abstract.

### Calendar Origin

The need for a calendar revealed a deeper political foundation.

The statue, the founder, Viriatus, House Ventari, and Regulatus began connecting through the question of whose death mattered enough to reset time.

The calendar became a form of propaganda: one date with different meanings depending on who reads it.

### Regulatus

Regulatus became clearer as emotional control.

It teaches people that obedience, gratitude, duty, and belonging are signs of worth.

This made Regulatus useful for NPC behavior because characters can act from the system they were raised inside.

### Founding Lines

The dynastic structure became four founding lines: Ermerus, Rutare, Ilunis, and Teresta.

Their initials form **ERIT**.

The line method became:

```text
experience -> emotional wound -> behavior -> historical path -> modern system
```

### Language Drift

The world began using Roman-derived language that had changed over time.

This kept the Roman root visible without making Erit's world feel like direct ancient Rome.

## Design Takeaway

The first day established the main worldbuilding direction: existing story details should create pressure, and that pressure should lead naturally into systems, history, and later player-facing choices.
